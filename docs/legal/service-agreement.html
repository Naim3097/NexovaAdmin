Terms & Conditions

  1. Scope of Services. Nexova will provide the services and deliverables described in Schedule 1 (the “Services”). Any work not expressly listed as included is out of scope and may be quoted separately. Items marked “Not Included” in the Quotation are excluded.
  2. Term & Commitment. This Agreement begins on the date of signing (or first payment, if earlier) and continues for the term stated in Schedule 1 (the “Term”), including any minimum commitment period. On completion of the Term the Agreement ends automatically, unless the parties agree in writing to extend or renew it.
  3. Fees, Ad Spend & Taxes.
    1. Fees and the payment schedule are as stated in Schedule 1. All amounts are in Ringgit Malaysia (RM) and are exclusive of SST (currently 8%) where applicable.
    2. Third-party media / advertising spend (e.g. Google, Meta) is paid directly to the platform on the Client’s behalf and is not a fee to Nexova. Any top-up above the included budget is funded separately by the Client.
    3. Invoices are due per Schedule 1. Nexova may suspend the Services while any undisputed invoice remains overdue.
  4. Client Responsibilities. The Client will provide timely access, content, brand assets, approvals and account access (e.g. domain, hosting, analytics, ad accounts) needed to perform the Services, together with a single point of contact for approvals. Delays in Client inputs may extend timelines without affecting fees.
  5. Approvals & Revisions. Deliverables are deemed approved if not rejected in writing within 5 business days. Reasonable revisions to bring a deliverable in line with the agreed brief are included; new requirements, added scope or a change of direction after approval may be quoted separately.
  6. Intellectual Property.
    1. Upon full payment for the relevant deliverable, ownership of the final, approved deliverables created specifically for the Client transfers to the Client.
    2. Nexova retains ownership of its pre-existing tools, templates, code libraries, know-how and methods, and grants the Client a licence to use them as embedded in the deliverables.
    3. Third-party assets (e.g. stock media, fonts, plugins) remain subject to their own licences.
    4. Nexova may reference the Client and display non-confidential work in its portfolio unless the Client opts out in writing.
  7. Confidentiality. Each party will keep the other’s non-public information confidential and use it only for this engagement. This obligation continues for two (2) years after the Term.
  8. Data Protection (PDPA). Both parties will comply with the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (Malaysia). Nexova will process personal data only to deliver the Services and will apply reasonable security measures.
  9. Results & Third Parties. Nexova applies professional skill and industry best practice but does not guarantee specific rankings, traffic, leads or sales, as these depend on third-party platforms and market factors outside our control. Nexova is not liable for changes to third-party platforms, algorithms, policies or outages.
  10. Limitation of Liability. To the extent permitted by law, neither party is liable for indirect or consequential loss. Nexova’s total liability under this Agreement is capped at the total fees paid by the Client in the 3 months preceding the claim (excluding ad spend).
  11. Suspension & Termination.
    1. Either party may terminate for material breach not remedied within fourteen (14) days of written notice.
    2. The Client may terminate for convenience after any minimum commitment on 30 days’ written notice.
    3. On termination, the Client pays for all Services delivered and work-in-progress up to the termination date. Prepaid milestone amounts for completed work are non-refundable.
  12. Force Majeure. Neither party is liable for delay or failure caused by events beyond reasonable control (e.g. natural disasters, outages, government action).
  13. Governing Law. This Agreement is governed by the laws of Malaysia, and the parties submit to the non-exclusive jurisdiction of the Malaysian courts. The parties will attempt good-faith resolution before commencing litigation.
  14. Entire Agreement. This Agreement and its Schedules constitute the entire agreement between the parties and supersede prior discussions. Variations must be in writing and signed by both parties. If any clause is unenforceable, the remainder stays in effect.
